The Unexpected Joy of Single-Purpose Apps

Currentwave · 11 March 2026

In an era of everything-apps, there's a growing appetite for software that does exactly one thing and does it well. A timer that just times. A note app that just takes notes. A weather app that just shows the weather.

The appeal isn't nostalgia. It's cognitive relief. Every feature added to an app is a decision the user didn't ask to make. Single-purpose tools respect the fact that your attention is finite and your patience for settings screens is even more so.

The best ones disappear after use. You open them, do the thing, and close them. No feed to scroll, no streak to maintain, no notification asking you to come back. Software as a tool, not a destination.

There's a business model question, of course. Single-purpose apps are harder to monetise because they don't demand daily engagement. But the developers building them have found that people will pay upfront for software that respects their time — and that a loyal user who paid once is worth more than a reluctant subscriber who cancels after the trial.
