Mechanical Keyboards Went Mainstream. Now What?

Currentwave · 17 March 2026

What started as a niche hobby for enthusiasts who debated switch actuation forces on forums has become a genuine consumer category. Major retailers now stock hot-swappable boards next to wireless mice and webcams.

The mainstreaming of mechanical keyboards has been a double-edged sword. Prices have dropped and build quality has improved across the board. But the community that spent years perfecting the craft worries that mass production dilutes the soul of what made custom keyboards special in the first place.

The truth, as usual, sits somewhere in the middle. More people typing on better hardware is unambiguously good. And for those who want to go deeper, the artisan end of the market has never been more inventive. Group buys for hand-polished aluminium cases and small-batch switches still sell out in minutes.

What's changed isn't the ceiling — it's the floor. The entry point used to be a $150 gamble on something you'd never tried. Now it's a $40 board from a brand you recognise, and if you like it, the rabbit hole is right there waiting.
