How a Tiny Studio in Porto Built the Year's Best Weather App

Currentwave · 13 March 2026

Three designers and two engineers in a converted sardine factory overlooking the Douro River have done what billion-dollar companies couldn't: make checking the weather feel delightful.

Their app, Céu, uses fluid animations that mirror actual atmospheric conditions. Fog doesn't just appear as an icon; it drifts across the interface. Rain doesn't ping a notification; it subtly shifts the colour temperature of the entire app. The result is information you feel before you read it.

The team spent four months on the rain animation alone. "We recorded actual rainfall against a window and traced the fluid dynamics frame by frame," says lead designer Ana Ferreira. "Then we abstracted it until it was simple enough for a phone GPU but still felt wet."

It's a reminder that taste and restraint can still outperform scale. Not every problem needs a hundred engineers. Sometimes it just needs five people who care deeply about getting one thing right.
