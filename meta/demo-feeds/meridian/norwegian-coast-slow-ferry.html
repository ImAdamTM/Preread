The Norwegian Coast by Slow Ferry

Meridian · 18 March 2026

The Hurtigruten ferry from Bergen to Kirkenes takes six and a half days. Flights cover the same distance in two hours. Choosing the ferry is choosing to be bored, cold, and astonished in roughly equal measure.

You eat breakfast watching the Lofoten Islands materialise from grey mist. You drink coffee while the ship threads through channels so narrow you could almost touch the rock face. You stand on deck at midnight as the sky does something with light that doesn't have a name in English.

The ship stops at thirty-four ports along the way, some for five minutes, some for an hour. At each one, a handful of passengers step off into fishing villages where the air smells of salt and wood smoke, and a handful more step on. The population of the ship turns over slowly, like a river.

Slow travel isn't about the destination. It's about letting the journey take up enough space in your memory that you actually keep it.
