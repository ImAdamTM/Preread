The Overnight Train Making a Comeback in Europe

Meridian · 9 March 2026

The sleeper train from Vienna to Paris departs at eight in the evening. You have dinner in the restaurant car somewhere over the Austrian Alps, fall asleep in Bavaria, and wake up pulling into Gare de l'Est with coffee and a croissant on your fold-down table.

A decade ago, night trains were a dying relic. Now they're the fastest-growing segment of European rail. New routes are launching every season, with modern carriages that make the old couchettes feel like a distant memory.

The compartments are small but thoughtfully designed. A fold-down bed, a reading light, a window that frames the passing darkness like a cinema screen showing a film with no plot but excellent cinematography.

The appeal isn't just environmental, though that matters. It's the romance of going to sleep in one country and waking up in another, with no security queues, no baggage carousel, and no lost hours in airport limbo.
