Why Your Sourdough Starter Deserves a Rest Day

Sated · 18 March 2026

The sourdough community has a tendency toward over-attentiveness. Twice-daily feedings, precise hydration ratios, temperature logs that rival a chemistry lab. But your starter is tougher than you think, and occasionally neglecting it might actually make it better.

A cold retard in the fridge for a day or two slows fermentation and develops deeper, more complex flavour compounds. The wild yeasts don't die; they just work more slowly, producing acetic acid that gives the bread a more rounded tang.

Professional bakers have known this for decades. The best boulangeries in Paris retard their dough overnight as a matter of course. The home baking community, fuelled by Instagram and a desire for control, somehow skipped this step in favour of more feeding, more monitoring, more anxiety.

Think of it as the bread equivalent of a rest day at the gym. The growth happens during recovery.
