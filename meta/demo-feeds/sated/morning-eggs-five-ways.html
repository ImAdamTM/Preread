Morning Eggs, Five Ways

Sated · 12 March 2026

An egg is a six-minute meal with a decade of depth. Here are five ways to cook one that feel completely different from each other, all using the same ingredient and a single pan.

Soft-scrambled. Low heat, constant stirring, a knob of butter, and patience. Pull them off the heat while they're still slightly wet. They'll finish cooking on the plate. Add chives at the end.

Olive oil fried. Get the oil hot enough that the edges bubble and crisp. The whites should lace like a doily. Season with flaky salt only.

Poached. Simmering water, a splash of vinegar, and a gentle swirl. Three and a half minutes for a runny yolk. Lift with a slotted spoon and drain on a towel.

Basted. Fry in butter, then add a tablespoon of water and cover with a lid. The steam cooks the top while the bottom stays crisp. Best of both worlds.

Shakshuka-style. Crack directly into a small pot of simmering tomato sauce with cumin and a pinch of sugar. Cover and cook until the whites set. Eat with crusty bread.

Master all five and you'll never be bored at breakfast again.
