# Preread — Marketing Plan

*Collaborative output from the marketing team: Maya (Marketing Lead), Jess (App Store SEO/ASO), and Kai (Design & Creative Direction).*

---

## 1. Team Discussion & Alignment

### Maya (Marketing Lead)
The positioning is clear: Preread is **not** a feed reader for power users. It's a personal reading app for anyone who wants a calm, curated alternative to social media noise. The key insight from user testing (the wife test) is spot-on — regular people don't know what RSS is, and they don't need to. They understand "add your favourite sites, read them beautifully, read them offline." That's the entire pitch.

Our three audiences, in priority order:
1. **Curious readers** — People who follow multiple interests (tech, food, travel, science) and are tired of algorithmic feeds. They want to choose what they read.
2. **Commuters & travellers** — Anyone who regularly finds themselves without internet and wants something to read. Planes, subways, remote areas.
3. **Intentional screen-time people** — The "digital wellness" crowd who want their phone to be a tool, not a slot machine.

We do NOT lead with "RSS" or "feed reader." Those words signal niche, technical, and ugly. We lead with the experience: beautiful, calm, offline, personal.

### Jess (App Store SEO/ASO)
Agreed on avoiding RSS in the headline, but we absolutely need it in the keyword field and lower in the description — people who *do* search for RSS readers are high-intent users who convert well. We can have it both ways: lead with the lifestyle pitch, include the technical terms where search engines look.

The App Store category should be **News** (not Utilities, not Productivity). News gets significantly more browse traffic, and the app fits the category perfectly.

The subtitle (30 chars) is the most important ASO real estate after the name. It needs to convey the core value and contain a searchable keyword.

### Kai (Design & Creative Direction)
The app already looks great from the screenshots — dark mode, clean typography, beautiful article images from the demo feeds. The design language sells itself. Our job is to frame it.

For App Store screenshots: no device bezels, no chrome. Full-bleed app UI with a single headline per screen. The app is the hero, not our marketing copy. Minimal text, maximum UI.

For the website: single-page, dark theme matching the app aesthetic. Hero section with a phone mockup, then scroll through features. No stock photos — everything should be app UI or the demo feed imagery (Unsplash, which is rights-cleared).

## 2. App Store Listing

### App Name
**Preread**

### Subtitle (30 characters max)
`Read offline. Beautifully.`

Alternatives considered:
- `Your articles. Always ready.` (29 chars)
- `Articles cached & offline` (25 chars)
- `Read anything, anywhere` (23 chars)

**Jess's note:** "Read offline" captures the primary value prop and contains two strong search keywords. "Beautifully" differentiates from utilitarian competitors.

### Keywords (100 characters)
`rss,reader,offline,articles,news,feed,read later,save articles,magazine,curator,reading,bookmarks`

**Jess's note:** Keyword field is comma-separated, no spaces after commas to maximise character use. Includes "rss" and "feed" for power-user discovery without surfacing those terms in visible copy. "read later" captures Pocket/Instapaper searchers. "magazine" and "curator" are low-competition, high-relevance.

### Category
**Primary:** News
**Secondary:** Productivity

### Description

```
All the things you love to read. In one place. Ready whenever you are.

Preread is your personal article reader. Add websites and publications you care about, and Preread visits them on your behalf — fetching, cleaning, and storing full articles so they're ready before you are.

A calm, focused reading experience. Just the words and images, beautifully presented.

— DISCOVER NEW INTERESTS —

Browse a curated directory of hundreds of sources across 40+ topics — from world news and science to food, architecture, photography, and beyond. Find publications you'd never have searched for on your own.

— ALWAYS READY, ALWAYS OFFLINE —

Preread works quietly in the background. It checks your sources, fetches full articles, and caches everything — including images — on your device. Open the app on a plane, in the subway, or anywhere without WiFi. Everything just works.

— A READING-FIRST EXPERIENCE —

• Beautiful reader — articles presented in a clean, focused layout
• Customisable fonts and text sizes
• Dark mode, light mode, or match your system
• Reading time estimates on every article
• Full-screen image viewer with pinch-to-zoom

— SET IT AND FORGET IT —

• Automatic background refresh keeps your library current
• WiFi-only mode to save mobile data
• Per-source settings for full control
• Drag and drop to reorder your sources

— EVERYWHERE YOU ARE —

• Home screen and lock screen widgets
• Apple Watch companion app
• Share extension — send any URL to Preread from Safari or any app
• Siri shortcuts — "Read Science in Preread"
• iPad split view support

— YOUR ARTICLES, YOUR WAY —

• Save articles to a personal collection
• Search across all your sources
• Swipe actions for quick read/unread, save, and share
• Share original links — always links back to the publisher

Preread respects your time, your attention, and the publishers who create the content you love. When you share an article, you share the original link. When you discover a new source, you're discovering a new website to support.

Beautiful reading. Quietly prepared. Always ready.
```

**Maya's note:** The description leads with emotion and benefit, not features. Technical details (background refresh, caching) are presented as user benefits ("everything just works"). The publisher-respect angle is at the end — subtle but present for anyone who reads that far.

**Jess's note:** Section headers use em dashes and caps for scannability in the App Store. Key search terms ("offline," "reader," "articles," "widgets," "dark mode") appear naturally. The first three lines are the most critical — they show before the "more" fold.

## 3. App Store Screenshots

### Strategy (Kai)
Six screenshots maximum. Each screen = one clear message + app UI. Dark backgrounds throughout to match the app's default appearance and stand out in the App Store (most apps use light backgrounds).

No device frames. Full-bleed app UI with a single line of text above or overlaid. The app speaks for itself.

Use the demo feeds (Currentwave, Sated, Meridian, Prism, Atelier) — they have beautiful Unsplash imagery and look like real publications without any trademark issues.

### Screenshot Sequence

**Screenshot 1 — Hero / Home Screen**
- Show: Home screen with "Preread for you" header, article carousel with a beautiful food photo (Sated), source sections below
- Text overlay: **"All your articles. Ready when you are."**
- Purpose: First impression. Shows the app is polished and content-rich.

**Screenshot 2 — Reader View**
- Show: Reader view of an Atelier or Meridian article with a stunning hero image, clean typography, dark mode
- Text overlay: **"Beautiful. Distraction-free. Offline."**
- Purpose: The core experience. Shows what reading actually looks like.

**Screenshot 3 — Browse Topics**
- Show: The topic directory screen with categories (World News, Tech, Science, Food, Travel, etc.)
- Text overlay: **"Discover something new."**
- Purpose: Shows breadth and discoverability. Signals this isn't just for tech people.

**Screenshot 4 — Add a Source**
- Show: The "Add a source" sheet with the search field, Find Articles / Save Single Page buttons, and Browse Topics link
- Text overlay: **"Add any website. We'll handle the rest."**
- Purpose: Shows simplicity of adding content. Demystifies how it works.

**Screenshot 5 — Settings / Customisation**
- Show: Settings screen with appearance toggle, reading font, text size slider, sync options
- Text overlay: **"Your reading. Your way."**
- Purpose: Shows polish and configurability. Dark/light toggle is visually compelling.

**Screenshot 6 — Widgets**
- Show: Home screen with Preread widgets (medium + small) alongside other apps
- Text overlay: **"Glance at your latest articles."**
- Purpose: Shows system integration. Widgets are a strong differentiator.

### Video Preview (optional but recommended)
15-20 seconds. No narration, subtle ambient music.

Sequence:
1. App opens to home screen (1-2s)
2. Scroll through home, showing sources and carousel (3s)
3. Tap into a Meridian article — reader view opens with a beautiful travel photo (3s)
4. Scroll through the article, showing clean typography (2s)
5. Swipe back, tap Browse Topics — show the category grid (2s)
6. Quick flash of a widget on the home screen (2s)
7. End on the app icon with tagline: "Preread. Ready when you are." (3s)

## 4. Website Plan — preread.app

### Structure (Single Page)

**Kai's direction:** Dark theme, matching the app. Minimal sections, generous whitespace, large typography. The phone mockup is the centerpiece. No feature grids — features are presented as short story beats as you scroll.

#### Section 1: Hero
- Large headline: **"All the things you love to read. In one place. Ready whenever you are."**
- Subheadline: "Preread is your personal article reader. It visits your favourite sites, fetches full articles, and stores them beautifully — so they're ready before you are."
- CTA: App Store download badge
- Visual: iPhone mockup showing the home screen (dark mode, demo feeds loaded)

#### Section 2: The Problem
- Headline: **"The internet is noisy. Your reading doesn't have to be."**
- Short paragraph about social media noise, information overload, and the feeling of never finishing an article
- Visual: Phone mockup showing the clean reader view with a beautiful article

#### Section 3: How It Works
Three steps, each with a phone mockup:
1. **"Add the sites you love."** — Screenshot of Browse Topics or Add Source sheet
2. **"We read them for you."** — Brief animation or illustration of background caching (abstract, not technical)
3. **"Read anywhere. Even offline."** — Screenshot of reader view, perhaps with an airplane mode icon visible

#### Section 4: Feature Highlights
Not a grid. A vertical scroll of 3-4 key features, each with a phone mockup:
- **"40+ topics to explore."** — Topics directory screenshot
- **"Beautiful, distraction-free reading."** — Reader view screenshot
- **"Widgets, Watch, and more."** — Widget screenshot or Watch app
- **"Dark mode. Obviously."** — Light/dark comparison of the same screen

#### Section 5: Closing CTA
- Headline: **"Preread is ready when you are."**
- App Store badge
- Small links: Privacy Policy, Support/Contact

#### Footer
- Copyright, Streamline Labs link
- Social links (if applicable)

### Prompt Kit for Website Build

Use these prompts to generate the website with an AI coding tool or frontend designer:

**Overall:**
> Build a single-page marketing website for an iOS app called "Preread." Dark theme (#0a0a0f background, #ffffff text, accent gradient from #4fd1c5 to #9f7aea). Single-page scroll. Minimal, editorial feel. Gabarito font for headings, system sans-serif for body. Mobile-first, fully responsive. No JavaScript frameworks needed — static HTML/CSS is fine.

**Hero section:**
> Hero section: large heading "All the things you love to read. In one place. Ready whenever you are." with a subheading underneath. Centered App Store download badge below. To the right (or below on mobile), a phone screenshot showing the app home screen. Dark background, generous vertical padding.

**Problem section:**
> Section with heading "The internet is noisy. Your reading doesn't have to be." followed by 2-3 short sentences. Subtle divider or gradient transition from the hero. No image needed — let the words breathe.

**How it works:**
> Three-step horizontal layout (stacked on mobile). Each step has a number, a short headline, one sentence of description, and a phone screenshot below. Steps: 1) "Add the sites you love" 2) "We read them for you" 3) "Read anywhere. Even offline."

**Features:**
> Alternating left-right layout: phone screenshot on one side, headline + short description on the other. Flip sides for each feature. Features: topic discovery, reader view, widgets, dark mode. Keep descriptions to 1-2 sentences max.

**CTA / Footer:**
> Closing section with centered heading "Preread is ready when you are." App Store badge. Below: minimal footer with privacy policy link, contact/support link, and copyright.
