Why You Should Be Making Stock on Sundays

Sated · 8 March 2026

A pot of stock takes five minutes of active work and four hours of doing nothing. You put bones, an onion, a carrot, a stick of celery, and cold water in a pot. You bring it to a gentle simmer. You walk away. That's it.

What you get back is a foundation for the entire week. Rice cooked in stock instead of water tastes like a different ingredient. A simple soup becomes something worth sitting down for. Even reheating leftovers improves when you splash in a few tablespoons.

The bones cost almost nothing — most butchers will give them away if you ask. Freeze the stock in ice cube trays and you have flavour on demand for a month. It's the highest-return, lowest-effort thing you can do in a kitchen.

Sunday is the ideal day because the house is already warm and unhurried. The stock simmers while you read, clean, or do nothing at all. By evening you have two litres of liquid gold and a kitchen that smells like someone's grandmother lives there.
