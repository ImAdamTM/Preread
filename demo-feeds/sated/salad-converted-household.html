The Salad That Converted a Meat-and-Potatoes Household

Sated · 10 March 2026

My father has eaten the same rotation of seven dinners for thirty years. Steak on Mondays, chicken on Wednesdays, fish on Fridays. Salad, in his worldview, is what food eats. So when he asked for seconds of this one, I knew I'd done something right.

The secret is treating the salad like a warm dish. Roast cubes of butternut squash and red onion until caramelised. Toss them while still hot over bitter leaves — radicchio, frisée, anything with backbone. Add toasted walnuts, crumbled blue cheese, and a dressing made from sherry vinegar, honey, and Dijon mustard.

The warmth wilts the greens just enough. The sweetness of the squash balances the bitterness of the leaves. It's a salad that earns its place at the centre of the table, not the side.

He still eats steak on Mondays. But now there's a salad next to it.
