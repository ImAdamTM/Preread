The Simplest Pasta You'll Make All Spring

Sated · 21 March 2026

Good pasta doesn't need a long ingredient list. This one has six things in it, and three of them are pantry staples you already own. The trick is letting each one do its job without getting in the way of the others.

Cook your spaghetti two minutes short of al dente. While it boils, warm good olive oil with thinly sliced garlic and a pinch of chilli flakes until the garlic just starts to turn golden. Toss the pasta in with a cup of starchy cooking water, a handful of torn basil, and a squeeze of lemon. Finish with pecorino.

The lemon is what makes this. It brightens everything without tasting citrusy. There's a moment when the starchy water emulsifies with the oil and the cheese, and the whole thing turns glossy and clings to the spaghetti like it was always meant to be there.

Serve it in a warm bowl and eat it before it cools down. This is a ten-minute meal that tastes like you spent an hour on it.
