A Guide to Seasoning Your Cast Iron (Without the Anxiety)

Sated · 16 March 2026

The internet has made cast iron care sound like a sacred ritual. It isn't. You need oil, heat, and a little patience. That's the whole secret.

Wipe a thin layer of flaxseed or grapeseed oil over every surface, inside and out. Place it upside down in a 250-degree oven for an hour. Let it cool completely in the oven. Do this three times for a new pan, and once every few months after that.

The most common mistake is using too much oil. You want a film so thin it's barely visible. Thick layers polymerise unevenly and create sticky patches that flake off. Less is genuinely more here.

The anxiety around cast iron is worse than the actual maintenance. Cook with it, clean it with hot water and a stiff brush, dry it on the hob, and move on with your life. The pan will outlast you either way.
