The Return of the Courtyard Home

Atelier · 15 March 2026

For centuries, from Roman villas to Moroccan riads, homes were built around open courtyards. The form created private outdoor space, cross-ventilation, and natural light without exposing the interior to the street. Then suburbia happened, and the courtyard was replaced by the front lawn.

Now, as urban plots get smaller and privacy becomes harder to achieve, architects are rediscovering the courtyard as a solution to a very modern problem: how to live outdoors in a dense city without being overlooked.

The new courtyard homes don't look like their historical predecessors. They're compact, often single-storey, and use the enclosed garden as a second living room. Sliding glass walls blur the boundary between inside and out. The courtyard becomes the heart of the house, visible from every room, changing with the seasons.

The principle is ancient and unchanged: the best outdoor space is the one that belongs only to you.
