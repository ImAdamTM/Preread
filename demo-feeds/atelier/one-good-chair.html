Designing a Room Around One Good Chair

Atelier · 9 March 2026

Interior design often starts with the room and works inward: walls, then furniture, then accessories. But some of the best spaces are designed in the opposite direction, starting with a single object and letting everything else respond to it.

A well-chosen chair anchors a room the way a thesis anchors an essay. It sets the tone, the palette, and the level of formality. Everything else in the space becomes a supporting argument.

The chair doesn't need to be expensive. It needs to be considered. A bent plywood Eames shell. A worn leather club chair from a flea market. A Hans Wegner Wishbone if you're feeling generous. Pick one, place it where it catches the light, and build outward from there.

The room that results will have something that rooms designed by committee rarely achieve: a point of view.
