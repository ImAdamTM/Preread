Your Phone's Best Feature Is the One You Never Use

Currentwave · 5 March 2026

Every phone shipped in the last three years has a Focus mode, a Screen Time tracker, and a Do Not Disturb schedule. Almost nobody uses them. The tools to reclaim your attention are already in your pocket — they're just buried three menus deep behind the apps designed to steal it.

Setting up a custom Focus mode takes five minutes and changes the entire character of your phone. A "Reading" focus that silences everything except calls. A "Morning" focus that hides social apps until noon. The phone becomes a different device depending on what you're trying to do.

The resistance isn't technical — it's psychological. We're afraid of missing something. But the math is simple: the average person receives 80 notifications a day. Each one takes roughly 20 seconds to process, even if you don't act on it. That's 25 minutes a day spent on context switches that you didn't choose and mostly don't need.

The irony is that the same companies selling you attention-recapture tools are also the ones selling your attention to advertisers. But the tools are real, they work, and using them doesn't require deleting anything. Just five minutes in Settings, and your phone starts working for you instead of against you.
