Mexico City's Best Neighbourhood Is the One You Haven't Heard Of

Meridian · 15 March 2026

Everyone goes to Roma and Condesa. They're beautiful, well-documented, and full of people who read the same travel guides. But twenty minutes south, Coyoacán moves at a different speed entirely.

The streets are cobbled and shaded by jacaranda trees that turn purple in March. The markets sell tlacoyos stuffed with fava beans and slathered in green salsa. The Frida Kahlo museum draws crowds, but two blocks away the neighbourhood empties out into quiet plazas where elderly couples share benches and ice cream.

On Sundays the main square fills with musicians, balloon sellers, and families eating elote from paper cups. The atmosphere is festive without trying to be. Nobody is performing for tourists because, for the most part, there aren't any.

The best version of any city is always one turn away from the popular version.
