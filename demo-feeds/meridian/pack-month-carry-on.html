How to Pack for a Month in a Carry-On

Meridian · 3 March 2026

The trick isn't packing less. It's packing smarter. After fifteen years of living out of a 40-litre bag for weeks at a time, the system is simple: five bottoms, seven tops, one layer, one pair of shoes you can walk ten miles in.

Everything should work with everything else. If a shirt only goes with one pair of trousers, leave it at home. Merino wool is your best friend — it regulates temperature, resists odour, and dries overnight on a towel rail.

Roll, don't fold. Use packing cubes not for organisation but for compression. Keep toiletries minimal — most places in the world sell shampoo. The things you think you'll need "just in case" are the things you'll carry for a month without touching.

The real liberation isn't at the airport. It's at every train station, bus stop, and cobbled street where you're not dragging a suitcase behind you like a penance.
