A Week on the Amalfi Coast, Off-Season

Meridian · 12 March 2026

In July, the Amalfi Coast is a postcard you can't enjoy because forty other people are standing in front of it. In November, it belongs to you.

The water is still warm enough to swim. The restaurants are open but unhurried. You can walk the Path of the Gods without queuing, and the light in the late afternoon turns the limestone cliffs the colour of honey.

Ravello is quietest. The gardens of Villa Cimbrone are empty enough to sit on the Terrace of Infinity and feel, for a moment, like the name isn't hyperbole. Below, the sea is the kind of blue that makes you understand why the Romans built villas here two thousand years ago.

Off-season travel requires accepting that some things will be closed and the weather might not cooperate. In return, you get the thing that no amount of money can buy in peak season: space.
